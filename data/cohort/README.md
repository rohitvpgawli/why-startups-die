# Cohort sampling frames

`yc-2021/` is a frozen sampling frame for the YC Winter 2021 and Summer 2021
batches. It is generated by `scripts/fetch_yc_2021_roster.py` from the public
[`yc-oss/api`](https://github.com/yc-oss/api) batch endpoints.

This is not yet the reviewed Startup Graveyard dataset. The roster contains
publicly launched YC companies and retains YC's directory status as a clue; it
does not prove that a company is active, dormant, or shut down. Batch year is
also not the same thing as founding year. Founding and first-funding dates must
be verified separately before a company enters a 2021 lifecycle denominator.

`founding-date-candidates.jsonl` is the automated extraction queue. The
hand-reviewed `founding-date-reviews.jsonl` records three accepted 2021 claims
and one rejected/conflicted claim; these are date reviews only and do not
assign lifecycle outcomes.

`roster-enriched.jsonl` applies those date decisions to a derived copy of the
roster. The frozen source snapshot remains unchanged, and the enriched copy
still has null lifecycle outcomes for every company.

`lifecycle-status-reviews.jsonl` records provisional operating evidence for
three verified 2021-founded companies. These records remain `needs_review` and
do not turn a live website, job listing, or YC status into a final outcome.

`sec-sale-candidates.jsonl` is an SEC Form D join queue covering the supplied
2018 Q1–2021 Q4 files. It contains exact normalized company-name matches, a
reported `SALE_DATE` candidate, earlier reported/amendment dates, and EDGAR
filing links for review. It intentionally leaves `first_funded_on` null: Form
D coverage is a financing signal, not proof of the first funding event or of a
complete funding history. The sidecar includes all 727 roster rows so
unmatched companies remain visible rather than disappearing from the audit.

`review-queue.jsonl` joins the roster, reviewed founding-date decisions, SEC
financing candidates, and provisional lifecycle signals into one row per
company. Its `eligibility_decision` is a workflow state, not an outcome; the
current queue has three reviewed right-censored operating records and no
publishable terminal outcomes. `review_priority` records 70 reviewed
identity/date cases as resolved; no pre-2021 or standard 2021 candidates
remain in the review-priority queue. Twelve name matches are explicitly
rejected, three are retained as predecessor relationships, and 55 are
accepted identity matches. These reviews resolve identity/date workflow
cases only; they do not promote any `SALE_DATE` to `first_funded_on`.

The manifest records retrieval time, source URLs, record counts, and SHA-256
hashes for the raw source files. Keep the raw snapshot with the roster so the
sampling frame remains reproducible even as the upstream directory changes.

Validate and summarize the frozen frame with:

```bash
python3 scripts/report_yc_2021_roster.py
```

To reproduce the financing candidate window, fetch the SEC ZIPs into a
temporary directory and then run the join:

```bash
python3 scripts/fetch_sec_form_d_data.py --start-year 2018 --end-year 2021
python3 scripts/build_sec_form_d_candidates.py \
  --zip-dir /tmp/formd-history \
  --coverage-label 'SEC Form D 2018 Q1 through 2021 Q4'
```

The report checks the manifest hashes and counts, then prints batch counts,
YC directory-status counts, missingness, lifecycle-field population, and (when
present) SEC candidate coverage. A
non-null lifecycle outcome is rejected from this staging roster; outcomes must
be separately reviewed and sourced before publication.
